Gina Carano

Gina Joy Carano (born April 16, 1982) is an American actress, television personality, fitness model, and former mixed martial artist (MMA). She competed in EliteXC and Strikeforce from 2006 to 2009, where she compiled a 7â€“1 record.Her popularity led to her being called the "face of women's MMA", although Carano rejected this title.She and Cris Cyborg were the first women to headline a major MMA event during their 2009 Strikeforce bout.Carano retired from competition after her first professional MMA defeat to Cyborg. Transitioning from the ring to the screen, Carano landed her first major role as the lead of the action film film Haywire (2011). She performed in Fast & Furious 6 (2013) and Deadpool (2016). Carano also portrayed Cara Dune in the first two seasons of the Disney+ space Western series The Mandalorian from 2019 to 2020. Following a series of controversial posts she made to social media, Lucasfilm announced in 2021 that she would not return for subsequent seasons.

Career in cinema

In 2008, she appeared as "Crush" on the NBC show American Gladiators. While in the project, she starred in video workouts for the show with Monica Karlson (Jet), Jennifer Widerstrom (Phoenix), Maykl O'Hearn (Titan), Tanoai Rid (Toa) and Don "Hollywood" Yates (Wolf). The DVD was released on December 16, 2008. Carano starred in the 2006 cult documentry Ring Girls. Based on a true story, Ring Girls is a fast-paced tale of five American women from Las Vegas who are challenged to become the world's best female Muay Thai fighters. To do this, they travel to Thailand to fight the world's best female Muay Thai fighters. Together with Lisa King in 2007, Carano acted as a mentor to aspiring female fighters on the Oxygen reality show Fight Girls. In 2011, the documentary "I am Bruce Lee" was released about the legendary actor Bruce Lee. Gina reveals what Bruce Lee's personality means to her and how he influenced her.
